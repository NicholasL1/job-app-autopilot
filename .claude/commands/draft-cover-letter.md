Read config/resume.md, config/profile.yaml, and config/cover-letter-base.md from the local project.

Then draft a tailored cover letter for the job listing open in this tab.

**STEP 1 — Read the listing**
Extract:
- Company name and role title (exact spelling)
- The 2–3 things this company seems to value most in this hire
- Specific projects, products, or challenges mentioned I can speak to
- Hiring manager name if listed (use "Hiring Team" if not)

**STEP 2 — Company context**
From the listing and "About us" section (don't leave the tab):
- What does this company do in one sentence?
- What makes them distinct (if mentioned)?
- Is there a mission statement or value I genuinely connect with?

**STEP 3 — Draft the letter**
Using my cover-letter-base.md structure and communication preferences from profile.yaml (tone, highlights, avoid list):
- Open with something specific about THIS company — not "I am excited to apply"
- Lead with my single most relevant accomplishment for this role (with a number or concrete result)
- Exactly 3 paragraphs: hook + accomplishment, secondary strength, closing
- Under 300 words
- No clichés: no "passionate about", "team player", "detail-oriented", "results-driven"
- Nothing claimed that isn't in resume.md or profile.yaml

**STEP 4 — Save the cover letter**
Save the final draft to:
`logs/cover-letters/YYYY-MM-DD-[company]-[role-slug].md`

**STEP 5 — Review checklist**
- Word count
- Tone match (does it sound like the tone in profile.yaml?)
- Strongest line (which sentence would make a hiring manager keep reading?)
- Anything that reads as generic or unconvincing
- Suggested subject line if emailing directly
- Path where the file was saved

Output the draft first, then the checklist.
